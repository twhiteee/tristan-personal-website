TRISTAN WHITEHOUSE

Product Designer with a Mechanical Engineering degree from Cornell University

CAREER INTERESTS

I am a Cornell University Mechanical Engineering graduate with a passion for product design and entrepreneurship. I am currently working on my startup: Shaky Suds. Shaky Suds is bringing to market a new, faster way to wash dirty dishes by hand for people who don't have a dishwasher.

Previous to Shaky Suds, I worked as a product research & design engineer at Johnson Controls. I worked for their retail solutions division, developing/launching new RFID sensors used to improve anti-theft and inventory management systems for retail stores.

Previous to Johnson Controls, I was a product development engineer at Amphenol SV Microwave, a manufacturer of connector, adapter, and cable assembly products for RF and Mil/Aero industries. In addition to product development, I developed assembly process automation machinery for production teams and equipment for testing new solutions. I even used my programming skills to automate repetitive engineering tasks across multiple departments.

Previous to SV, I was a design engineer at Hog Technologies, a leading manufacturer of vehicles that develop/maintain roadways and airport runways. My work focused on development of the Thermo Hog – A thermoplastic longline truck used to paint roadway markings.

Previous to HOG, I worked as a mechanical engineering intern at Air Techniques, a global manufacturer of medical devices for the dental industry. I was responsible for​ packaging design, prototype, and testing initiatives for Air Techniques' novel digital radiography solutions.

My interest in design started during my cumulative nine months interning as a programmer and supply chain analyst for Lufthansa Technik's Consumables and Expendables department. I was responsible for developing/implementing software that streamline and automate company procedures/reports.

My experience designing multiple hardware and software solutions has taught me a lot about myself. Coming up with product ideas has shown me how uniquely I view the world and its many inefficiencies. Brainstorming different designs has shown me how creative and inventive I am. Successfully turning these ideas into physical, working prototypes and final products proves my ability to get things done and create real-world impact.

I am excited to build upon my experiences so I can achieve my goal of having a fulfilling career making products that people love.
